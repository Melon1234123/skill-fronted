# Source fingerprint

The visual language changes with the narrative phase instead of repeating one static treatment across the page. Media and evidence lead: imagery, recordings, measurements, and demonstrations establish the claim before supporting copy explains it.

Use one narrow attention colour for the primary action, selected state, or critical signal. Keep a quiet, continuous background behind readable scrims and opaque proof surfaces so that atmosphere never competes with the message.

Headline lines enter first. Supporting reveals then follow the reader's natural reading order, using calm motion that clarifies sequence rather than decorating the screen.

Reduced motion, dependable fallbacks, cleanup after transitions, and performance discipline are visual-quality requirements. A presentation is not finished if its motion, media, or loading behavior makes the evidence harder to read.
